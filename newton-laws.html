Newton's First Law of Motion: The Law of Inertia

Newton's First Law describes an object's resistance to changes in its state of motion. An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion remains in uniform motion unless acted upon by a net external force.

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same velocity unless acted upon by a net force.

  • Rest: Stationary objects remain still without external force.
  • Motion: Moving objects continue moving uniformly unless a force changes their motion.
  • Inertia: The resistance to change is proportional to the object's mass.

Key Terms for First Law

  • Inertia: Resistance to changes in motion.
  • Force: A push or pull (measured in Newtons).
  • Net Force: The total force acting on an object.
  • Equilibrium: A state where the net force is zero.

Newton's Second Law of Motion: The Law of Acceleration (F = ma)

Newton's Second Law quantifies the effect of force on motion by relating net force, mass, and acceleration.

F = m * a
  • F = Net Force (N)
  • m = Mass (kg)
  • a = Acceleration (m/s²)

Essentially:

  • Acceleration is proportional to Force: More force produces greater acceleration.
  • Acceleration is inversely proportional to Mass: More mass means less acceleration with the same force.
  • Direction: Acceleration occurs in the direction of the net force.

Key Terms for Second Law

  • Acceleration: The rate of change of velocity (m/s²).
  • Mass: The amount of matter, affecting resistance to acceleration (kg).
  • Weight: Gravitational force on mass (N), where Weight = m * g.

Newton's Third Law: Action-Reaction & Elastic Collision

Newton's Third Law states that for every action there is an equal and opposite reaction. This simulation demonstrates a nearly elastic collision where two spheres collide and exchange momentum.

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction.

  • Interaction: In a collision, objects exert equal and opposite forces.
  • Equal Forces: The forces exchanged are equal in magnitude.
  • Opposite Directions: The forces act in opposite directions.
  • Momentum Exchange: In an elastic collision, momentum and kinetic energy are exchanged.

Key Terms for Third Law

  • Action Force: The force exerted by one object on another.
  • Reaction Force: The equal and opposite force returned.
  • Elastic Collision: A collision where objects rebound with minimal energy loss.
  • Momentum: The product of mass and velocity.
  • Kinetic Energy: The energy of motion.
